QUESTIONS

1 : What is the role of Incoterms in international trade?

Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) are standardized trade terms used in international contracts to define the responsibilities of buyers and sellers regarding the delivery of goods.

02 : What is a Letter of Credit, and how does it work in trade finance?

A Letter of Credit (L/C) is a financial instrument issued by a bank guaranteeing that a buyer's payment to a seller will be received on time and for the correct amount.

03 : What is the difference between a tariff and a quota in international trade?

A tariff is a tax imposed by a country on imported goods, making them more expensive and less competitive compared to domestic products.

04 : What are some of the common risks faced in export trade, and how can they be mitigated?

Export trade involves several risks, including political risks (such as changes in government policies or sanctions), economic risks (such as currency fluctuations or inflation), and payment risks (such as non-payment by the buyer).
